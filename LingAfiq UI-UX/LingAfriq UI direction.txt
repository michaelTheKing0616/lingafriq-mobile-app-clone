Below is a **creative, story-driven, culturally rich, and cleverly data-gathering onboarding process** for an *African-language learning app*. It not only feels immersive and fun, but also collects **all key information** needed to personalize a world-class learning experience‚Äî**age, proficiency, goals, dialect preference, pacing, schedule, learning style, accessibility needs, motivation triggers, social features, and more.**

# üåç **‚ÄúThe Journey Begins‚Äù ‚Äî An Ingenious African Language App Onboarding Flow**

### **Concept:**

The user enters a virtual African village called **‚ÄúKijiji cha Lugha‚Äù** (The Language Village).
Every essential onboarding question is embedded into a **storyline**, where the user interacts with beautifully animated ‚Äúguides‚Äù representing different African cultural archetypes.

This ensures *engagement*, *emotional bonding*, and *zero feeling of interrogation*‚Äîyet the app gathers all the critical data for personalized learning.

---

# üß≠ **Step 1 ‚Äî Meeting the Village Elder (Warm Welcome + Age + Culture)**

### **Experience:**

A calm, wise Elder appears under a giant baobab tree.

**The Elder says:**
‚ÄúWelcome, traveler. In our village, every learner has a story. Tell me yours, so I may guide your path."

### **Data Collected:**

‚úî Age range (child, teen, adult)
‚úî Cultural familiarity with Africa (optional)
‚úî Whether learning for personal, heritage, or academic reasons

### **Mechanics:**

The elder presents scrolls with choices‚Äîeach scroll beautifully illustrated.

---

# üïä **Step 2 ‚Äî The Dialect Weaver (Language Choice + Dialect + Proficiency)**

### **Experience:**

A youthful character weaving kente or ankara patterns on a loom.

**The Weaver says:**
‚ÄúEach thread represents a dialect, each color a voice. Which would you like to weave into your speech?‚Äù

### **Data Collected:**

‚úî Language selection (Swahili, Yoruba, Amharic, Zulu, etc.)
‚úî Sub-dialects/regional variants
‚úî Current proficiency (absolute beginner ‚Üí fluent)
‚úî Literacy preferences (spoken only, spoken + written)

### **Mechanics:**

Colorful cloths represent dialects; selecting one opens a short, optional ‚Äúproficiency mini-quiz.‚Äù

---

# ü•Å **Step 3 ‚Äî The Rhythm Master (Learning Style)**

### **Experience:**

A drummer invites the user to ‚Äúfind their rhythm.‚Äù

### **Data Collected:**

‚úî Preferred learning style (audio, visual, stories, repetition drills, conversation)
‚úî Sensitivity to background music or sound effects
‚úî Pace preference (slow, steady, fast, ‚Äúsurprise me‚Äù)

### **Mechanics:**

The user taps different drums that play learning samples (audio lesson, quiz snippet, visual flashcard).
What they tap most determines their learning style.

---

# ‚è∞ **Step 4 ‚Äî The Timekeeper (Schedule + Commitment Level)**

### **Experience:**

A figure with a beautifully crafted African sundial.

**The Timekeeper asks:**
‚ÄúEven the sun keeps a schedule. What about you?‚Äù

### **Data Collected:**

‚úî Daily/weekly availability
‚úî Ideal lesson length (5‚Äì25 minutes)
‚úî Preferred study session times
‚úî Whether they want reminders

### **Mechanics:**

Choose sunrise, midday, sunset, or moonlit night‚Äîeach corresponds to specific scheduling templates.
Users can customize.

---

Paths include:

* üõ´ *Travel Path*
* ‚ù§Ô∏è *Heritage & Family Path*

‚úî Primary learning goals

---

# üé® **Step 6 ‚Äî The Personality Griot (Tone + Gamification + Personalization)**

### **Experience:**

A griot holding a talking drum asks about the user‚Äôs personality.

### **Data Collected:**

‚úî Preferred app tone (playful, formal, humorous, gentle)
‚úî Whether they like competition (leaderboards)
‚úî Preference for cultural content (proverbs, songs, folktales, history)
‚úî Whether they want culturally immersive visuals

**Mechanics:**

---

# üå± **Step 7 ‚Äî The Village Health Keeper (Accessibility)**

### **Experience:**

A healer checking the user‚Äôs ‚Äúlearning health.‚Äù

### **Data Collected:**

‚úî Vision accommodations (large text, high contrast)
‚úî Hearing preferences
‚úî Dyslexia-friendly settings
‚úî No-sound mode
‚úî Motion sensitivity

# ü§ù **Step 8 ‚Äî Build Your Clan (Social learning)**

### **Experience:**

### **Data Collected:**

‚úî Whether the user wants:

  ‚úî Comfort level with speaking practice

---

# üåü **Step 9 ‚Äî The Naming Ceremony (Profile Creation)**

### **Experience:**

The village celebrates the learner by ‚Äúgiving their learning identity.‚Äù

### **Data Collected:**

‚úî Username
‚úî Avatar (stylized African art characters or real photo)
‚úî Optional location (for cultural tailoring)

---

# üöÄ **Step 10 ‚Äî The First Step on the Path (Personalized Starter Test)**

### **Experience:**

The user is guided through a fun, short **placement journey**‚Äîmatching words to objects, selecting correct tones, or responding to simple greetings.

### **Data Collected:**

‚úî Real proficiency assessment
‚úî Vocabulary baseline
‚úî Pronunciation ability (if microphone allowed)

# **üìÑ Product Requirements Document ‚Äî ‚ÄúKijiji cha Lugha Onboarding‚Äù**

### **1. Product Summary**

Kijiji cha Lugha is an African language-learning app that uses a culturally immersive narrative onboarding to collect detailed learner data for hyper-personalized lessons.

---

### **2. Objectives**

* Create a high-engagement onboarding experience
* Collect key personalization data without friction
* Reduce 7-day churn through emotional bonding
* Enable algorithm-driven customization based on user inputs

---

### **3. Personas**

**Primary:**

* Adults (18‚Äì45) learning African languages for travel, heritage, business
* Teens seeking school support
* Diaspora communities seeking reconnection

**Secondary:**

* Tourists
* Professionals working across Africa
* Linguistic hobbyists

---

### **4. Onboarding Data Inputs**

#### **User Profile**

* Age category
* Username
* Avatar

#### **Language Profile**

* Language choice
* Dialect preference
* Proficiency level
* Literacy vs oral preference

#### **Learning Preferences**

* Learning style
* Speed
* Tone (playful/serious/etc.)
* Gamification level
* Cultural content preference

#### **Motivation & Goals**

* Primary goal
* Secondary goals
* Motivation style

#### **Schedule**

* Daily duration
* Preferred time
* Reminder preference

#### **Accessibility**

* Large text
* High contrast
* Dyslexia mode
* Sound sensitivity
* Motion reduction

#### **Social Preferences**

* Solo vs community
* Competition preference
* Speaking practice comfort

---

### **5. User Journey (10-Step Flow)**

1. Arrival scene
2. Elder introduction
3. Dialect Weaver
4. Rhythm Master
5. Timekeeper
6. Path Chooser
7. Griot
8. Healer
9. Naming ceremony
10. Placement test

---

### **6. Functional Requirements**

* Multi-scene onboarding flow
* Animated or illustrated characters
* Dynamic question logic
* Placement test engine
* Preference-based theme selection
* Save and resume

---

### **7. Non-functional Requirements**

* Load time < 2 seconds
* Offline-friendly after first load
* Accessible to WCAG AA
* Localization for multiple languages

---

### **8. Success Metrics (KPIs)**

* Onboarding completion rate > 85%
* Day 7 retention +20%
* Lesson engagement time +30%
* Accuracy improvement in first 14 days

---

### **9. Risks & Mitigation**

* Too long onboarding ‚Üí break into chapters
* Overwhelm new users ‚Üí use story + visuals to reduce friction
* Low literacy users ‚Üí audio narration
